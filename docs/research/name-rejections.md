# Name rejection ledger

Status: preliminary naming research, 2026-07-11. No candidate in this document is approved for public branding, packages, repositories, deployments, domains, or social accounts.

## Non-negotiable exclusions

- **Slick** remains user-rejected. **Parrot** was initially rejected, then explicitly selected by the
  user as the public product name on 2026-07-12. Cosmetic misspellings such as Parrott or Parrotly
  remain rejected. The reversal is a product decision, not professional trademark clearance.
- Names built around generic `AI`, `agent`, `chat`, `team`, `flow`, `sync`, or `hub` language were rejected unless unusually distinctive. None survived.
- Deliberate misspellings whose only purpose is identifier availability were rejected.
- Names that read as imitations of Slack, Discord, Reddit, X/Twitter, Teams, or Notion were rejected.

## Process and threshold

The private generation pass produced more than 170 one-word candidates across conversation, gathering-place, durable-memory, weaving, social-animal, coordination, and compound-name territories. The pool below records the discarded field. A name was eliminated for any one of: direct or close product use, crowded software use, weak pronunciation or spelling, misleading meaning, genericness, unwanted product associations, or poor icon/mascot potential.

This is a rejection ledger, not a trademark opinion. Exact and similar marks can coexist or conflict depending on jurisdiction, goods/services, priority, and consumer-confusion analysis.

## Registry and directory limitations

- The USPTO trademark search interface was reachable, but its client-rendered results were not reliably extractable in this environment. Exact-name searches through public trademark indexes did not surface exact federal records for the four finalists, but that is not equivalent to an official clearance search.
- Publicly indexed checks against WIPO Global Brand Database and EUIPO/TMview surfaces did not surface exact finalist hits. Index coverage and jurisdictional data are incomplete; phonetic, design-mark, translation, and owner-family searches remain necessary.
- Florida's Sunbiz entity search was blocked by an anti-bot challenge, and Delaware's entity interface did not provide a reliable unauthenticated batch result. No state-registry conclusion should be drawn.
- App-store, package, organization, domain, and social identifiers can change at any time. Absence of an exact result is not ownership, registrability, or freedom to use.
