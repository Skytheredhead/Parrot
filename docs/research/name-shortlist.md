# Preliminary name shortlist

Status: decision input only, researched 2026-07-11. Continue to use the neutral internal codename `project-conversation`. Do not use any candidate below in public branding, repository names, package names, deployments, domains, logos, or marketing until the user selects it and counsel clears it.

## Recommendation

**Advance Threadroost first**, with Gatherfold as the strongest non-bird-adjacent alternative.

Threadroost best preserves the useful energy of the rejected Parrot direction—social, colorful, talkative, memorable, and mascot-friendly—without naming another bird. It also maps cleanly to the product: a roost is a recognizable gathering place, while the product gathers bounded threads beneath durable posts. Among the finalists it had the best exact-identifier signal in this pass, including no Verisign RDAP record for the exact `.com` at the time checked.

That recommendation is conditional. `Thread` is a crowded software element, and nearby products such as Threadkeep, Threadhall, Threadmark, Threadnest, and PostRoost make a professional similarity search essential.

## Shortlist

### 1. Threadroost

- **Pronunciation:** `THRED-roost`
- **Meaning:** a welcoming place where conversation threads gather and remain findable. It quietly supports the post → bounded threads model without sounding like enterprise workflow software.
- **Speech test:** “Put it in Threadroost.” “I’ll start a post in Threadroost.” “The agent replied in Threadroost.” All are understandable on first hearing.
- **Icon and mascot potential:** a compact woven-roost mark made from two or three conversation strands; an expressive roost-builder mascot; colorful eggs/cards as posts with bounded thread strands beneath them. The mark can remain legible at favicon size without copying a bird silhouette.
- **Exact-use screen:** no exact product, app, package, or organization use surfaced in general web searches; exact npm package and exact GitHub owner checks returned no record; Apple's U.S. software search returned no exact title. Google Play search did not expose an exact titled listing in the inspected results.
- **Related conflicts:** the `Thread-` field is crowded. [Threadkeep](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/vinaypatankar_i-shipped-my-first-open-source-project-today-activity-7464084767513399296-Ncdn) is a Discord/agent conversation orchestrator, [Threadhall](https://www.threadhall.net/) is a structured-conversation workspace, [Threadmark](https://www.threadmark.app/) has discussion and AI-memory products, Threadnest appears in forum projects, and [PostRoost](https://postroost.com/) organizes social posts. Those are not exact matches, but they matter to confusion analysis.
- **Trademark screen:** no exact `THREADROOST` hit surfaced through the public trademark indexes checked for USPTO/WIPO/EUIPO. This is a weak negative signal, not registry confirmation. A professional search must cover `THREAD ROOST`, `THREADROOST`, `THREAD*ROOST`, phonetic equivalents, design marks, and classes 9, 38, 41, 42, and relevant SaaS/community services.
- **Identifier plausibility:** strongest of the set. Verisign RDAP returned no record for the exact `.com` when checked. Exact npm and GitHub-owner identifiers returned no record. `.app`, `.chat`, `.team`, and `.social` showed no DNS answers, which is only a routing observation—not proof of registration availability. Social handles were not authenticated or reserved.
- **Preliminary risk:** **moderate**. Low exact collision signal, but a crowded `Thread-` neighborhood and descriptive undertone.

### 2. Gatherfold

- **Pronunciation:** `GATH-er-fohld`
- **Meaning:** people, posts, and agent work are gathered, then folded into bounded, inspectable discussions. It is also a real production term for gathering multiple sheets before folding, which gives it a durable publishing/post metaphor rather than a made-up spelling.
- **Speech test:** “Share it in Gatherfold.” “Open the Gatherfold post.” “We resolved that in Gatherfold.” Clear, though slightly more editorial than social.
- **Icon and mascot potential:** folded conversation cards forming a circle; an origami creature assembled from post and thread shapes; a strong `G/F` fold mark for small icons.
- **Exact-use screen:** no exact communication/collaboration product surfaced. Exact npm package, GitHub owner, and U.S. Apple software-title checks returned no record.
- **Related conflicts:** `GatherFold` is an established JDF/digital-printing term used in [Canon documentation](https://docs.cpp.canon/help?pageid=GUID-50E5FA82-2C59-4C4E-A784-77B750DE21CD.xml&tsm=ODP000136-7.4EN.GB) and the [CIP4 specification](https://www.cip4.org/files/cip4/documents/Integrated%20Digital%20Printing%20ICS%201.8.pdf) for multi-sheet finishing. That creates search noise and may make the name descriptive around document-production software. The exact `.com` is registered.
- **Trademark screen:** no exact finalist mark surfaced through the public trademark indexes checked, but the ordinary technical use makes a registrability/descriptiveness review especially important. Search `GATHERFOLD`, `GATHER FOLD`, `FOLDGATHER`, and `GATHER + FOLD` variants.
- **Identifier plausibility:** exact npm and GitHub-owner identifiers returned no record; no exact U.S. Apple software title was found. The exact `.com` is registered, while the checked alternate TLDs had no DNS response. A modifier or negotiated domain may be needed.
- **Preliminary risk:** **moderate**. Better category separation than Threadroost, but weaker domain position and meaningful technical-term baggage.

### 3. Threadkin

- **Pronunciation:** `THRED-kin`
- **Meaning:** the humans and agents connected through a shared thread. `Kin` makes the name social and inclusive without using generic team or AI language.
- **Speech test:** “Ask in Threadkin.” “The production crew is on Threadkin.” “Invite the agent to Threadkin.” Short and natural.
- **Icon and mascot potential:** a family of small knot/strand characters—human and non-human participants with the same visual grammar; interlocking loops that form a friendly face or group mark.
- **Exact-use screen:** no exact communication/collaboration software surfaced. Exact npm package, GitHub owner, and U.S. Apple software-title checks returned no record.
- **Related conflicts:** [Threadkins](https://threadkins.com/pages/our-story) is a current handmade crochet character/flower brand, and `threadkin` appears as an individual Hugging Face/PennyLane handle. [`THREADKIND` has a recent U.S. application](https://trademarks.justia.com/998/48/threadkind-99848588.html) in textile goods and current nonprofit/commerce uses. The exact `.com` is registered. The plural and `kind` variants need careful phonetic review even though the goods differ.
- **Trademark screen:** no exact `THREADKIN` record surfaced through the public indexes checked. That does not dispose of Threadkins, Threadkind, unregistered use, or international records.
- **Identifier plausibility:** exact npm and GitHub-owner identifiers returned no record; no exact U.S. Apple software title was found. Exact `.com` is registered; checked alternate TLDs had no DNS response. Social handles were not authenticated or reserved.
- **Preliminary risk:** **moderate-high**. Excellent semantic and mascot fit, but adjacent plural/phonetic commerce uses reduce confidence.

### 4. Gatherkind

- **Pronunciation:** `GATH-er-kynd`
- **Meaning:** gather people of every kind—and, by extension, humans and agents—around durable posts. It also reads as “gather kindly,” giving the product an approachable community tone.
- **Speech test:** “Post it to Gatherkind.” “Our club uses Gatherkind.” “The agent joined Gatherkind.” Easy to hear and spell.
- **Icon and mascot potential:** a circle of distinct companion figures; several differently shaped marks gathering around one post; broad palette and character-system potential without forcing a specific animal.
- **Exact-use screen:** no exact software or app use surfaced. Exact npm package, GitHub owner, and U.S. Apple software-title checks returned no record.
- **Related conflicts:** [Gatherkind is an active-name Etsy vintage/home-goods shop](https://www.etsy.com/uk/shop/Gatherkind) with documented sales, creating real common-law use. `Gather` alone is highly crowded in software and events. The exact `.com` is registered.
- **Trademark screen:** no exact federal/international result surfaced through the public indexes checked, but the exact common-law use means this candidate requires more than registry searching.
- **Identifier plausibility:** exact npm and GitHub-owner identifiers returned no record; no exact U.S. Apple software title was found. Exact `.com` is registered; checked alternate TLDs had no DNS response. Handle availability remains unverified.
- **Preliminary risk:** **high end of moderate**. Strong speech and audience breadth, weaker ownership position.

- Exact, spaced, compound, related, and obvious phonetic web queries.

- Exact npm Registry package checks.
- Exact GitHub owner and repository checks.
- Apple's U.S. software search and Google Play web-search inspection.

- Publicly indexed USPTO, WIPO Global Brand Database, and EUIPO/TMview checks, supplemented by public U.S. trademark indexes.
- Attempted state-entity checks; Florida was blocked by an anti-bot challenge, and Delaware did not yield a reliable batch result.

## Required next step before selection becomes a brand

This research is **not legal clearance** and does not establish ownership, registrability, or freedom to use. After the user selects one or two candidates:

1. Have qualified trademark counsel run a comprehensive U.S. and international knockout/full search, including phonetic, design, translation, state, company-name, app, domain, and common-law sources.
2. Recheck all identifiers live and purchase/claim them only after approval.
3. Run a short spoken-name test with people outside the project: hear → spell, read → pronounce, and “what kind of product is this?”
4. Keep `project-conversation` in every public-facing identifier until those gates pass.
