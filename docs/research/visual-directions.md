# Visual direction options

Status: requires user selection, 2026-07-11

These directions are paired with the three IA models for the first visual decision. A user may later combine a product model with a different visual system.

## Direction A — Social paper

- **Paired model:** Living posts.
- **Typography:** humanist sans for UI and a restrained editorial serif for post titles only.
- **Density:** medium; substantial visible post bodies, generous reading measure, compact metadata.
- **Navigation:** slim workspace/space index, wide feed, no floating app card and no Slack-like channel rail.
- **Feed composition:** posts form a continuous reading surface separated by rules and whitespace; thread previews are inset rows, not cards inside cards.
- **Color:** warm paper neutrals, dark ink, muted coral and cobalt for actions/status; light and dark themes retain the paper/ink relationship.
- **Mobile:** full-width content, minimal chrome, sticky post identity, bottom navigation, no desktop card treatment squeezing the viewport.
- **Strength:** most approachable across sports teams, schools, communities, and companies.
- **Failure risk:** can look like Facebook or a lifestyle publishing product if outcomes and agent runs lose visual clarity.

## Direction B — Operational signal

- **Paired model:** Signal desk.
- **Typography:** precise grotesk sans with a mono companion for state, time, command hints, and agent receipts.
- **Density:** high but disciplined; flat rows, aligned columns, strong keyboard focus, few decorative surfaces.
- **Navigation:** command menu plus compact index; selection and responsibility drive the screen more than persistent channel navigation.
- **Feed composition:** an ordered work table with one expanded post; thread/status rows use rules and typographic weight instead of pills or cards.
- **Color:** graphite, cool white, electric blue, and restrained amber/red only for pending/risk; dark theme is native, not inverted decoration.
- **Mobile:** finite queue first, large tap rows, one expanded context at a time, bottom-sheet commands only when necessary.
- **Strength:** best for production and software teams that need fast scanning, keyboard control, and auditable agents.
- **Failure risk:** can become Linear-like, intimidating, or too corporate for friend groups and student organizations.

## Direction C — Civic briefing

- **Paired model:** Daily briefing.
- **Typography:** accessible contemporary sans with a distinctive soft display face for section headings.
- **Density:** low to medium; finite sections, clear reading progress, editorial hierarchy, strong empty space.
- **Navigation:** workspace masthead, edition date/catch-up selector, concise space directory; canonical posts stay one tap away.
- **Feed composition:** newspaper-like section rhythm without tiny columns; `Needs you`, `Decisions`, and `Around your spaces` use shared flat surfaces and lightweight dividers.
- **Color:** off-white, deep indigo, saffron, and plum/green accents; colorful but not childish, with AA contrast.
- **Mobile:** single-column daily edition, anchored source links, exact return position, prominent live-activity escape hatch.
- **Strength:** calm, memorable, and unusually friendly for async communities.
- **Failure risk:** can understate urgency or make live work feel secondary; generated summaries can appear more authoritative than sources.

## Recommendation

Advance **Direction A — Social paper** first. It makes the post primitive immediately understandable without borrowing an incumbent's distinctive trade dress. Incorporate Direction B's crisp agent receipts and Direction C's finite digest typography as supporting components, not competing visual systems.
